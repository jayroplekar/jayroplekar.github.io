Task-Level Analysis

Administrative and Clerical

Easy-to-learn

  • Data entry and verification.
  • Scheduling appointments and managing calendars.
  • Answering routine emails and phone calls.
  • Creating and formatting standard documents.
  • Processing invoices and expense reports.

Hard-to-learn

  • Handling complex and non-routine inquiries.
  • Negotiating with vendors and clients.
  • Organizing and planning complex events.
  • Providing personalized assistance to executives.
  • Resolving interpersonal conflicts within the office.

Procurement

Easy-to-learn

  • Creating purchase orders from templates.
  • Tracking and expediting orders.
  • Verifying receipt of goods and services.
  • Processing payments to vendors.
  • Maintaining and updating supplier databases.

Hard-to-learn

  • Developing and implementing procurement strategies.
  • Negotiating complex contracts with suppliers.
  • Identifying and evaluating new suppliers.
  • Managing supplier relationships and performance.
  • Resolving disputes with suppliers.

Software Development

Easy-to-learn

  • Writing boilerplate code and simple functions.
  • Generating unit tests for well-defined functions.
  • Debugging simple and common errors.
  • Creating documentation from code comments.
  • Performing simple code refactoring.

Hard-to-learn

  • Designing complex software architectures.
  • Developing novel algorithms and data structures.
  • Solving complex and poorly defined problems.
  • Optimizing code for performance and scalability.
  • Collaborating with other developers on large and complex projects.

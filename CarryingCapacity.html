The Carrying Capacity Problem

It all started with an innocent and friendly debate with @Srujan, my mathematician/scientist in training son posing two critical questions upon we watching an interesting documentary over the winter break: The Exposing The Dark Side of America's AI Data Center Explosion | View From Above | Business Insider

The documentary talks about the exponential growth of AI datacenters in US, their voracious appetite for power and water, and the environmental impact of these datacenters. And fundamentally questions the sustainability of this growth. The two fundaental questions that the documentary posed in Srujan's view:

  1. Is there a "known sustianable limit" for power/water consumption for datacenters in the US ?
  2. Are the big techs essentially absolving themselves of responsibility by using water and carbon credits for others to do the hardwork and possibly at irrelevant places.

My Take

  1. An idealistic view: All rapidly growing technologies test the limits of sustainability. Businesses have to work within current infrastrucutre and legal frameworks. As the limitations of existing frameworks due to growth become apparent regulators catch up and incentivize or regulate the "right behavior" to keep the impact of the innovations beneficial. In this case (Data center growth), the big players (Google, Amazon, Meta) are staying ahead of the curve and creating incentives by using credits which will lead to the right solutions to emerge.
  2. A pragmatic view: The big players are doing what is legal and within their fiduciary responsibility to their shareholders. If the regulators do not like it, they should change the rules of the game.
  3. A more mathenatically inclined view (Srujan's): There is a "Carrying Capacity Problem" here, and we need to explore it further
Me not having heard of the "Carrying Capacity Problem" earlier, offered to be the research assistant to Srujan to explore this further. So here it goes ... hopefully, I will be a good student and learn something new along the way.
